Projects

March Madness - 2020

In the Spring of 2020, I took a class on Machine Learning, and for our final project, we were required to use advanced techniques to figure out some difficult problem. Naturally, being a sports fan, I tackled one of the most difficult projects: predicting March Madness results. Using data from 2003-2017, we evaluated numerous statistics to determine how to most effectively use those statistics to produce future results. Then, we tested the best results on the data from 2018 to determine the effectiveness of each model. Attached is a PDF of our final report, which shows that after testing, we approached the optimal results attained in several scientific articles published.

College Fantasy Football - 2019

In the Fall of 2019, I took a Website Development course. Similar to my Machine Learning course, we were required to create a final project that encapsulated various web-dev techniques. Once again, my partner and I took the sports route, creating a website that allowed users to manage their own college fantasy football team. Implementing RESTful APIs that contained real world statistics and storing them in data stores proved to be a difficult task, as we had to manage and collect data in creative ways. Additionally, we created a user login system, basic AIs to compete against (specifically in drafting), implemented techniques such as debouncing and autocomplete suggestions. While still a work in progress, attached is a video displaying how to use the program.
